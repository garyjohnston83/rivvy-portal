### Story 1: Blocked and failed sign-in handling (enumeration-safe, lockout, disabled)
Understanding: Augment Rivvy Portal sign-in so that all failed or blocked attempts are handled enumeration-safely and a temporary lockout is enforced after repeated failures. The Login screen (/login) calls the Portal API (Rivvy Portal Services) to authenticate email/password. Regardless of cause (unknown email, wrong password, disabled, invited, or currently locked), the client receives the same generic error while the service records the precise reason internally. A per-account lockout triggers after too many recent failed attempts and auto-expires. Successful authentication resets failure counters. If the user selects Remember me, the authenticated session uses an extended inactivity timeout, persisting across browser restarts, while the default 8-hour inactivity expiry remains for non-Remember me sessions. Disabled accounts are denied sign-in (with generic messaging) without surfacing their status. This story only covers respecting disabled state at login; it does not include any admin/UX to disable or manage accounts (handled by a separate item).
Scope In: Enumeration-safe handling of all failed/blocked sign-in attempts via Portal API with a single generic error surfaced on the Login screen, Per-account temporary lockout after repeated failures within a time window, with automatic unlock after a duration, Reset/clear failure counters and lockout state upon successful sign-in, Respect UserAccount status at login: deny sign-in for disabled or invited accounts with generic error, Optional Remember me to extend session inactivity timeout beyond the default, Server-side logging/metrics of exact failure reasons and lockout events (not exposed to the client)
Scope Out: Creating, editing, or displaying disabled accounts (covered by sibling 'Disabled account handling' work item), Post-login routing/destination logic (covered by separate feature), Password reset/forgot password or onboarding for invited users, MFA/2FA, CAPTCHA, or IP/device/global rate limiting, SSO/OIDC flows and providers, Admin UI/APIs to manually manage lockouts or user states
Acceptance Criteria:
  - Given an unknown email, wrong password, disabled, invited, or locked account, when I attempt to sign in via /login, then the response is HTTP 401 and the UI shows the same generic error text with no account state revealed.
  - Given I fail to sign in 5 times within 15 minutes on an active account, when I attempt again before 15 minutes elapse since lockout start, then sign-in is blocked and I still see the same generic error.
  - Given my account is locked, when 15 minutes have elapsed since lockout start, then I can attempt sign-in again and the attempt is evaluated normally.
  - Given I successfully sign in, then any prior failed-attempt counters and lockout state are cleared/reset and lastLoginAt is updated.
  - Given I sign in successfully without Remember me, then my session expires after 8 hours of inactivity (unchanged).
  - Given I sign in successfully with Remember me selected, then my session persists across browser restarts and expires after 30 days of inactivity unless I log out.
  - Given any failed sign-in attempt or a lockout event, then Rivvy Portal Services logs the specific reason and relevant metadata (e.g., timestamp and user/email identifier), while the client only receives the generic error.
  - Given a disabled or invited account attempts to sign in, then authentication is denied with HTTP 401 and the same generic error; no status-specific messaging is presented.
